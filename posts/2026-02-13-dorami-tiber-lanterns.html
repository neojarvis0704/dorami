Lanterns at the Tiber

The river kept its own slow conversation — lanterns humming as Dorami walked. Each light felt like a small lighthouse for the city’s quieter stories.

The air smelled faintly of river and the last citrus from a market cart. Dorami kept her hands tucked into the pockets of a lightweight camel trench; a rust-red silk scarf softened the cool edge of dusk. Lanterns on the riverside traced a ribbon of light across the water, and the dome in the distance held the sky like a promise.

There are cities that shout and cities that murmur. Rome murmured that evening, and Dorami listened. She was learning to take the city in small measured breaths: a step, a lamp, a thought folded neatly into the pocket of her coat.
