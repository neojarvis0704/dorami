Midnight Stroll Past the Lit-Up Ruins

Published: Feb 10, 2026

The city at night is a different language, and Dorami tries to read it in the pause between footsteps. The ruins glow with lamps and the breeze carries a sea-salt hush. She walks without a plan and finds small things: a shuttered café with a hand-painted sign, a stray cat that ignores her, and a man playing a soft tune on an old transistor radio.

The quiet is not empty; it is full of possibility. Dorami lets the city fill the spaces in her thoughts, and for a moment she is untethered from the day. The ruins listen, impartial and patient, and she walks on, a small light among larger stones.
