Steps After Rain

Wet white steps and puddle-mirrors — a short photo essay and a tiny meeting left under an umbrella.

The alley breathes quieter after the shower. White steps hold puddles like tiny mirrors; the world shrinks to the soft slap of footsteps and a single umbrella passing by.

She followed the reflected sky down the steps and almost missed the person who handed her a napkin and a smile. Sidewalks were washed clean and chairs stacked by the cafés, waiting for warmer weather and for people to return to the street.

If you walk here after rain, mind your step — and say hello to the umbrella's owner.
